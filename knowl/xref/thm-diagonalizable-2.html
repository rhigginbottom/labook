Theorem 6.3.10.

Let \(A \in M_n(\ff)\) have distinct eigenvalues \(\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_p\text{.}\)
  1. For \(1 \le k \le p\text{,}\) the dimension of \(\mathrm{eig}_{\lambda_k}(A)\) is less than or equal to the (algebraic) multiplicity of \(\lambda_k\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. The matrix \(A\) is diagonalizable if and only if the sum of the dimensions of the eigenspaces equals \(n\text{,}\) and this happens if and only if (i) the characteristic polynomial factors completely into linear factors, and (ii) the dimension of \(\mathrm{eig}_{\lambda_k}(A)\) equals the multiplicity of \(\lambda_k\) for each \(k\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  3. If \(A\) is diagonalizable and \(\mcb_k\) is a basis for \(\mathrm{eig}_{\lambda_k}(A)\text{,}\) then \(\mcb = \mcb_1 \cup \cdots \cup \mcb_p\) is an eigenvector basis for \(\ff^n\text{.}\)
