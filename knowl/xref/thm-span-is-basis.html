Theorem 5.3.15.

Suppose that \(V\) is a vector space with \(\dim(V) = n > 0\text{.}\) If \(B = \{\bfv_1, \ldots, \bfv_n \}\) is such that \(V = \spn(B)\text{,}\) then \(B\) is a basis for \(V\text{.}\)

Proof.

By The Spanning Set Theorem (TheoremÂ 5.2.12), we know that a subset \(B'\) of \(B\) will be a basis for \(V\text{.}\) But since \(\dim(V)=n\text{,}\) the size of \(B'\) must be \(n\text{.}\) Therefore, \(B'=B\) and \(B\) is a basis for \(V\text{.}\)
