Example 2.1.6.

The set of rational numbers \(\qq\) is a field. The rational numbers are defined as the set of all quotients (hence the symbol \(\qq\)) of integers; more formally,
\begin{equation*} \qq = \left\{ \frac{a}{b} \mid a, b \in \zz, b \neq 0 \right\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
We do not need to show that all of the field axioms hold in order to prove that \(\qq\) is a field. Since \(\qq\) is a subset of \(\rr\text{,}\) and since it therefore inherits its operations from \(\rr\text{,}\) some of the field axioms hold automatically.
