Theorem 5.5.3.

Let \(V\) be an \(n\)-dimensional vector space over \(\ff\text{,}\) and let \(\mcb\) be a basis for \(V\text{.}\) Consider the coordinate map \(C_{\mcb}:V \to \ff^n\) given by
\begin{equation*} C_{\mcb}(\bfv) = [\bfv]_{\mcb}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Then \(C_{\mcb}\) is an isomorphism.

Proof.

The function \(C_{\mcb}\) is a linear transformation. (We ask the reader to verify this in the exercises.) We note that \(C_{\mcb}\) maps the basis vectors in \(\mcb\) to the standard basis in \(\ff^n\text{.}\) So, by TheoremÂ B.0.2, \(C_{\mcb}\) is an isomorphism.
