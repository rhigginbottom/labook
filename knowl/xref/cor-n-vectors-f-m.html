Corollary 5.1.13.

If \(n > m\text{,}\) then every set of \(n\) vectors in \(\ff^m\) is linearly dependent.

Proof.

Let \(V' = \{\bfv_1, \ldots, \bfv_n\}\) be a set of vectors in \(\ff^m\) and let \(A\) be the matrix with the elements of \(V'\) as its columns. By Corollary 1.3.7 (or, technically, by the version of this result generalized to any field \(\ff\)), we know that the \(m\times n\) linear system represented by \(A\bfx = \bfo\) cannot have a unique solution. Since \(\bfx = \bfo\) is a known solution, the presence of another solution means that the columns of \(A\) must be linearly dependent by Proposition 5.1.11.
