Theorem 6.3.7.

Let \(A \in M_n(\ff)\text{.}\) If \(A\) has \(n\) distinct eigenvalues, \(A\) is diagonalizable.

Proof.

If \(A\) has \(n\) distinct eigenvalues, let \(\bfv_1, \ldots, \bfv_n\) be eigenvectors which correspond to those eigenvalues. Then, by Theorem 6.1.13, the set \(V' = \{\bfv_1, \ldots, \bfv_n \}\) is linearly independent. Since our vectors are in \(\ff^n\text{,}\) by Theorem 6.3.2 this basis of eigenvectors of \(A\) means that \(A\) is diagonalizable.
