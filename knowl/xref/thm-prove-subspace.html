Theorem 2.4.3.

Let \(V\) be a vector space over the field \(\ff\) and let \(W\) be a subset of \(V\text{.}\) Then \(W\) is a subspace of \(V\) if the following properties hold.
  1. For all \(\bfu, \bfv \in W\text{,}\) \(\bfu + \bfv \in W\text{.}\)
  2. For all \(c \in \ff\) and all \(\bfv \in W\text{,}\) \(c\bfv \in W\text{.}\)
  3. The zero vector \(\mathbf{0}\) of \(V\) belongs to \(W\text{.}\)
